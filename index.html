Introduction

Fishes are aquatic vertebrates found in nearly every body of water. They breathe through gills, move with fins, and are covered in scales. With over 34,000 species, they play crucial roles in ecosystems and human life.

Did you know?

Some fish can change color to match their environment, while others migrate thousands of kilometers between fresh and salt water.
